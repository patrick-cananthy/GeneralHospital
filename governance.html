Organizational Structure

The Governance Structure of TGH showcases a clear hierarchy and distribution of responsibilities across various departments and roles. At the top of the chart is the Medical Director, who serves as the Chief Executive Officer. The Medical Director is responsible for overseeing all operations and activities within the hospital, ensuring that the hospital runs efficiently and effectively to provide high-quality healthcare services.
Reporting directly to the Medical Director is the Internal Audit function. This unit is tasked with ensuring compliance with internal controls, policies, and regulations. The Internal Audit team conducts audits to safeguard the hospital’s integrity and accountability, ensuring that all operations adhere to established standards and procedures.
The Human Resource Management department also reports to the Medical Director. This department is crucial for managing staff recruitment, training, and overall human resource policies. By ensuring a well-functioning and motivated workforce, the Human Resource Management department supports the hospital’s mission and goals.The Hospital Administrator, also referred to as the Head of Hospital Administration and Support Services (HASS), oversees several operational departments. These include Estates, which manages hospital properties and facilities,and Transport, which handles the logistics and transportation needs. Procurement & Stores is responsible for acquiring and storing supplies, while Laundry & CSSD takes care of laundry services and the Central Sterile Services Department. The Catering department provides food services for patients and staff, and the Social Welfare department addresses the social needs of patients. The Mortuary manages mortuary services, and the Environment department ensures a clean and safe hospital environment.

The Clinical Coordinator also reports to the Medical Director and is responsible for the clinical operations of the hospital. This role includes overseeing the Clinical Heads of Departments, who lead various clinical departments. The Clinical Coordinator also manages the medical workforce, which includes medical staff, house officers (interns), and medical students, ensuring that clinical services are delivered efficiently and effectively.
The Head of Pharmacy reports to the Medical Director and oversees the Pharmacy and Dispensary Staff. This team is responsible for medication management and pharmaceutical services, ensuring that patients receive the correct medications in a timely manner and that all pharmaceutical operations comply with regulatory standards.
The Head of Nursing also reports to the Medical Director and manages the nursing department. This includes Ward In-Charges, who lead different nursing wards, and the nursing workforce, which comprises nursing staff, midwives, auxiliary staff, and nursing students. The Head of Nursing ensures that all nursing services are delivered with the highest standards of care and professionalism.
Lastly, the Head of Finance reports to the Medical Director and supervises the finance staff. This department is responsible for the hospital's financial operations, including budgeting, accounting, and financial planning. By managing the hospital’s finances effectively, the Head of Finance ensures that the hospital remains financially stable and capable of providing high-quality healthcare services.

With the introduction of the National Health Insurance Scheme (NHIS), hospital attendance surged dramatically, with outpatient visits increasing from 180,914 in 2007 to 207,329 in 2008. However, the hospital’s ability to handle the increased demand has been strained by several challenges. These include insufficient staffing, inadequate accommodation for employees, poor infrastructure with structural defects, and a lack of security personnel. Additionally, the hospital’s mortuary was shut down due to its deteriorated condition, and the absence of a pathologist has complicated autopsy procedures. Financially, NHIS reimbursement delays and the use of district-level tariffs for claims further strain the hospital’s resources.
Despite these challenges, Tema General Hospital continues to evolve to meet the healthcare demands of its patients. It currently operates with twelve wards, a bed capacity of 439, and provides 24-hour specialist and general services to both outpatients and inpatients. The hospital offers a wide range of services, including Internal Medicine, General Surgery, Obstetrics and Gynaecology, Paediatrics, Reproductive and Child Health Care/Family Planning, and Emergency Services . Additionally, it runs specialized clinics such as Eye Care, Dental, Diabetic Clinic, Intensive Care, Dermatology, ENT, Chest, Hypertensive, and Anesthetic Clinics, as well as the ART/PMTCT Unit.
Support services such as Laboratory/Blood Bank, Radiology/Ultrasound, Pharmacy, Physiotherapy, and ECG, along with facilities including a Staff Rest Room, Catering, Mortuary, Laundry/CSSD, Stores and Supplies, Estate, Social Welfare, Mental Health Unit, and Wellness Centre, ensure comprehensive care delivery.
